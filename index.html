Song Cycle Commission

During the early part of this year I will be composing a song cycle and collaborating with two talanted artists in the process. Mandi Barrus is a Mezzo Soprano that most recently earned her MM in vocal performance at the University of Utah. I will be setting some texts from lyricist Liz Christensen. The texts focus on themes of self-awareness, love, loss, and mental health. There is a recital scheduled for the fall that will feature the premiere of this and a few other works. Stay tuned for more info about performance dates and venues.

Mezzo-soprano Mandi Barrus is a musician originally from Seattle, Washington. With her "impressive technique and clear tone" , Ms. Barrus' performances are described as "a breath of fresh air" and "particularly impressive." She is known for her versatility of performance, with credits in both musical theater (Hale Centre Theatre, Utah Repertory Theatre Company, Centerpoint Legacy Theatre, The Grand Theatre) and opera (Utah Opera, La Musica Lirica, Utah Lyric Opera, Lyric Opera Ensemble, Idaho Falls Opera Theatre). A winner of Utah Opera's AriaFest and of the University of Utah's Concerto Competition, Ms. Barrus holds a Bachelor of Musical Arts degree with a minor in Theatre Arts from BYU-Idaho and a Master of Music degree from the University of Utah. Ms. Barrus shares her love of music as the Artistic Director of Opera Contempo , as a private voice teacher, and by presenting opera outreach programs in elementary schools throughout Utah, where she lives with her three children.
Liz Christensen is the producer and host of both the entertainment podcast "In the Telling" and the webseries "She Made Me Do It." She is also a playwright, editor, theatre director, choreographer, stage and screen actress, and Instagram poet. Her creative work includes nine feature films, three short films, three music videos and over 70 theatrical productions. She is the mother of a teenager and a tween, a caregiver of an elderly parent, and has been married to her college sweetheart for over a decade. She was born and raised along the Wasatch Front and received her BFA from the University of Utah.

Chamber Opera Commission

I will be composing a chamber opera to be premiered either in March 2021 or Fall 2021. The Libretto will be written by the extremely talented Jennifer Campbell, who I have previosly worked with on the song cycle 'Habit'. I can't reveal too much about the plot at this point, but it centers around technology and what it means to be human. Look for updates and insights about the process on my blog as the year progresses!

Jennifer Campbell is a writer with a background in history, journalism and video. Her articles have appeared on Faithcounts.org and in Utah Stories Magazine. She is currently working on a fantasy novel set in alt-world ancient Persia. When not writing she's busy getting called "noob" by her 11-year-old son, escaping to the Uintas and ripping it up on the flag football field.

Opera Contempo Composer-in-Residence

I have accepted an invitiation to be the first composer-in-residence for Opera Contempo. I look forward to helping to grow this important new outlet for modern voices and stories. One of the goals of Opera Contempo is to create space for new composers to debut new works about contemporary topics.

About Me

I am a composer currently living in Salt Lake City, Utah. From the first time that I wrote a simple piano piece I have been fascinated that a person can create something so beautiful, ugly, heart-wrenching, silly, sad, intricate or any other myriad descriptors from combining musical symbols together with whatever is in their imagination and knowledge. Over the years I have had the priveledge to study music more formally under composers whose music and knowledge I greatly admire, including Leslie Sommer, Roger Briggs, Sheila Silver, and Perry Goldstein. My personal style is heavily influenced by being introduced to such a wide variety of classical,popular, and experimental music within the context of their lessons and courses. Not only did these lessons give me a level of competency with the essential skills of orchestration, music theory, and musical analysis, but they also inspired me to understand new ways of organizing sounds and musical ideas.I've had experience in teaching, conducting, vocal performance, audio mixing, and countless other musical disciplines. Each of these endeavors has increased my respect for those who specialize in these areas, but also has helped me to realize that in the end I just truly love the act of creating and sharing music.

As I was finishing up my graduate studies in composition it became clear to me that a career as a music professor just wasn't something that appealed to me. I respect those who have the passion to teach, but it wasn't my passion, and certainly wasn't a career that I could see myself in long-term. From that point I decided to find ways to balance writing music with making a living.

This site represents that evolving idea. I've always loved film music and the collaborative aspect of combining so many artistic and creative disciplines into one project. The media music on this site represents some of my first steps in that direction. I plan to update it with more tracks and film projects as they present themselves. I will be writing production tracks and custom underscore. I believe that the skills I've acquired through formal and informal study have lead to the ability to craft imaginative music in service of a filmmaker's vision. I love mixing and exploring different stylistic idioms and constantly adding tools to my composer belt.

I continue to love and compose concert music. I'm always looking for ways to collaborate with talented artists of all genres. I believe in always progressing and finding new ground to develop a unique compositional voice. The concert music on this site is part of what will be a life-long journey to explore, compose and share the excitement that I felt writing my first piece of music. I'm excited for new projects that are in development, and I hope that you come back often to listen to new pieces and updates.

Outside of music I have several interests and hobbies. I am learning web development. This site is one of my first major projects! Much like creating music from symbols, I find great satisfaction in creating elements from simply typing in commands and code. I am an avid weight-lifter, I enjoy playing most sports, attending theatre and cultural events, reading and writing escapist literature, and most recently I've found a love for dancing. Whether or not people enjoy watching me dance is another matter entirely.
